# Company: Amazon

## Interview Focus

Amazon behavioral interviews evaluate candidates through the Leadership Principles. Strong answers should be concrete, metric-backed, and specific about the candidate's personal decisions, tradeoffs, and ownership.

Use STAR: Situation, Task, Action, Result. Keep the setup brief, spend most of the answer on actions and tradeoffs, and quantify the result.

## Principles Or Competencies

### Customer Obsession

Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. Show decisions driven by customer trust, safety, quality, reliability, usability, cost, or long-term user value.

### Ownership

Leaders think long term, act beyond their own team, and never say "that's not my job." Show responsibility in ambiguous ownership situations and durable fixes.

### Invent And Simplify

Leaders innovate and simplify. Show a simpler design, improved mechanism, useful invention, or externally inspired idea that reduced complexity.

### Are Right, A Lot

Leaders have strong judgment, seek diverse perspectives, and work to disconfirm their beliefs. Show evidence-based decisions and changed assumptions.

### Learn And Be Curious

Leaders are never done learning. Show curiosity, self-directed learning, experimentation, and how new knowledge improved the outcome.

### Hire And Develop The Best

Leaders raise the performance bar and develop others. Show coaching, mentoring, knowledge transfer, or helping teammates grow.

### Insist On The Highest Standards

Leaders raise the bar and ensure defects do not get passed downstream. Show quality ownership, defect prevention, testing, or operational excellence.

### Think Big

Leaders create and communicate bold direction. Show work that looked beyond the immediate task, scaled an idea, or served future customers.

### Bias For Action

Speed matters, and many decisions are reversible. Show calculated risk-taking, fast learning, and action despite incomplete information.

### Frugality

Leaders accomplish more with less. Show resourcefulness under constraints, cost-aware design, or simpler solutions without unnecessary spend.

### Earn Trust

Leaders listen, speak candidly, treat others respectfully, and are vocally self-critical. Show trust-building through transparency and accountability.

### Dive Deep

Leaders operate at all levels and stay connected to the details. Show debugging, metric investigation, root-cause analysis, or auditing assumptions.

### Have Backbone; Disagree And Commit

Leaders respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree, then commit once a decision is made. Show principled disagreement and follow-through.

### Deliver Results

Leaders focus on key inputs and deliver with quality and timeliness despite setbacks. Show measurable outcomes under pressure.

### Strive To Be Earth's Best Employer

Leaders create safer, more productive, higher performing, more diverse, and more just work environments. Show inclusion, team health, or better working mechanisms.

### Success And Scale Bring Broad Responsibility

Leaders understand that decisions have broader societal impacts. Show responsible thinking about safety, privacy, fairness, trust, or downstream consequences.

## Question Themes

Common Amazon prompts include:

- Tell me about a time you worked backward from a customer need.
- Tell me about a time you took ownership of something outside your role.
- Tell me about a time you simplified a process or system.
- Tell me about a time you made a difficult decision with incomplete information.
- Tell me about a time you raised the quality bar.
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with a decision and committed afterward.
- Tell me about a time you delivered results despite setbacks.
- Tell me about a time you failed or were wrong.

## Story Selection Notes

Choose stories with clear metrics, specific personal actions, and strong follow-up depth. Avoid vague team-level answers. For each story, prepare the decision path, alternatives, risks, data used, result, and lesson learned.
