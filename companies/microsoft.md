# Company: Microsoft

## Interview Focus

Microsoft behavioral interviews evaluate how candidates learn, collaborate, make decisions, and connect technical work to customer impact. Strong answers should show more than a successful outcome: they should explain what changed, what the candidate learned, how feedback shaped the approach, and how the work helped users or partner teams.

Use a STAR(R) structure for Microsoft answers:

- **Situation**: Set the scene briefly.
- **Task**: State the candidate's specific responsibility.
- **Action**: Spend most of the answer on decisions, collaboration, tradeoffs, and execution.
- **Result**: Quantify the outcome where possible.
- **Reflection**: Explain what was learned, what would be done differently, and how the experience shaped future behavior.

## Principles Or Competencies

### Growth Mindset

Microsoft emphasizes moving from a "know-it-all" mindset to a "learn-it-all" mindset. Interviewers look for examples where the candidate learned from mistakes, handled feedback, adapted after new information, and grew through challenge.

### Customer Obsession

Interviewers look for examples where technical decisions were driven by end-user impact, not just engineering elegance. Strong answers connect architecture, quality, cost, reliability, or delivery tradeoffs back to customer value.

### Inclusive Collaboration

Microsoft looks for candidates who can drive results through collaboration, not just individual execution. Strong answers should show the candidate's own contribution while also showing how they listened, included others, elevated teammates, and improved the team's outcome.

### One Microsoft

Interviewers value cross-functional thinking and the ability to break down silos. Strong answers should show how the candidate worked across teams, aligned stakeholders, shared context, or designed handoffs that helped other groups succeed.

### Collaboration

Communicating effectively within and across teams.

### Drive For Results

Working tenaciously to deliver, seeking bigger challenges, and holding yourself accountable.

### Customer Focus

Empowering every person and organization to achieve more.

### Influencing For Impact

Persuading and influencing others through effective communication.

### Judgment

Scoping complex problems and using business acumen to make knowledge-based decisions.

### Adaptability

Dealing with ambiguous and uncertain situations with agility.

## Notes

Common Microsoft behavioral themes include collaboration, ambiguity, growth mindset, customer impact, influence, judgment, and learning from feedback. Avoid repeating the same story across a loop when possible, and prepare follow-up details about tradeoffs, metrics, lessons learned, and what would be done differently.
